# AI Fundamentals Glossary Notes
*Source: IBM SkillsBuild - AI Fundamentals Course*

## Key Terms

**Artificial Intelligence (AI)**
The ability of machines to perform tasks that usually require human intelligence.

**Classical Machine Learning**
A group of models where computers use mathematical calculations to identify patterns in data.

**Decision Tree**
A classical ML model that splits data into branches to reach decisions.

**Decision-making AI**
Evaluates multiple possible actions and chooses the optimal action based on factors.

**Generative AI**
Systems that use deep learning models and datasets to create original content.

**Hallucination**
A response from a foundation model that includes off-topic, repetitive, or incorrect information.

**Linear Regression**
A ML model that recommends continuous outcomes by fitting a straight line to data.

**Machine Learning**
A subset of AI that helps computers learn from experience.

**Neural Network**
A computer system composed of layers of interconnected units (nodes) designed to mimic the brain.

**Reinforcement Learning**
A ML model where a tool learns to make decisions by trying actions and receiving feedback.

**Robustness**
A deep learning model's ability to maintain accurate performance when presented with new data.
